# About me
I am a postdoc in the Ergonomics department at the [Leibniz Research Centre for Working Environment and Human Factors](https://www.ifado.de/de/forschung/ergonomie/team/julian-reiser). I earned my Phd at the Faculty of Psychology at the Ruhr University Bochum, [Institute for Cognitive Neuropsychology](https://www.ikn.psy.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/indexE.html).

# Research Interests
My research focuses on cognitive processes related to modern working environments, especially those that involve motion and cognition simultaneously. Here, I am especially interested in the question how we can design work to meet the demands of workers. To get insights into these pressing questions, I apply methods from cognitive neuroscience and experimental psychology - e.g. response behavior, EEG, ECG, and eye-tracking - to get a holistic picture of underlying cognitive mechanisms.

I strongly believe in open science practices. Science should be open and accessible to anyone. In the end, this will increase the credibility, trustworthiness, and societal impact of our research. 

In case you have questions or comments regarding my research, don't hesitate to DM me on LinkedIn or send an email to (reiser[at]ifado.de).
